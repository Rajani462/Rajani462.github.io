PTSTORM Project

Extreme Precipitation Response to Temperature Variations

The Role of Storm Types and Global Circulation Patterns (MSCA 2025–2027)

The PTSTORM project aims to improve our understanding of the relationship between extreme precipitation and atmospheric temperature. It explores how different storm types and large-scale atmospheric circulation patterns influence precipitation intensity and scaling behavior.

Objectives

  • Classify and characterize storm events globally using satellite observations.
  • Analyze temperature–precipitation scaling across regions and storm types.
  • Assess the role of large-scale circulation patterns in modulating precipitation extremes.

Methodology

PTSTORM integrates satellite rainfall datasets (e.g., GPM IMERG), reanalysis products, and statistical models to derive scaling relationships and identify process-based drivers of extreme events.
